## Topic

Daily low-dose aspirin: evolving evidence for prevention vs bleeding risk

## Research Summary

The post references personal daily 81 mg aspirin use despite shifts in general recommendations; topic requires evidence-based nuance by age/risk profile.

## Key Findings

- USPSTF 2022 advises against starting aspirin for primary prevention at age 60+ and recommends individualized decisions for 40-59 with elevated CVD risk.
- Cardiology guidance distinguishes primary vs secondary prevention; aspirin remains standard in many secondary-prevention settings.
- Bleeding risk is the main trade-off and can outweigh benefits in lower-risk populations.

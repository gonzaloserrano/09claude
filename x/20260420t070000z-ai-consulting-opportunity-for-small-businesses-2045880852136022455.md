## Topic

A claim that uneven AI adoption among small businesses creates a real consulting opportunity, though the delivery model is harder than the post implies.

## Research Summary

- The SBA materials support the broad premise that small firms are adopting AI more than before, even if larger firms still lead.
- That makes the opportunity directionally credible for consultants or service operators targeting practical AI implementation.
- The weak point is not demand alone, but whether projects can be packaged, sold, and supported profitably at small-business price points.

## Key Findings

- The SBA says large firms have outpaced adopting AI into business practices, but small firms have recently closed the AI use gap.
- A real market signal does not automatically translate into easy recurring revenue for consultants.
- The tweet's implied path to effortless high income is anecdotal and skips acquisition, scope, and support costs.

## Practical Relevance (for Gonzalo)

- Useful if Gonzalo is evaluating AI services aimed at SMBs and wants a more sober read than social-post optimism.
- The main takeaway is to think in terms of repeatable offers and support burden, not just market excitement.

## Risks / Caveats

- The post is anecdotal and does not establish pricing power or retention economics.
- Small-business adoption can still be shallow, experimental, or highly budget-sensitive.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. Treat SMB AI consulting as a packaging and operations problem, not just a trend bet.
2. Test narrow offers with clear ROI before assuming retainer scale.
3. Use the SBA material as market context, not proof of easy consultant margins.
